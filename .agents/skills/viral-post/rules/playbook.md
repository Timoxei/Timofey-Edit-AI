# Viral X Post Playbook for Nate Friedman

Based on analysis of 31 viral posts from @WallStreetApes (15 posts, up to 1.19M views) and @LauraLoomer (16 posts, up to 3.69M views).

## The Golden Rule

> **Posts with RECEIPTS (names, dollar amounts, org connections, documents) get shared 2x more than posts with just reactions or interview clips.**

## Voice & Perspective

- Always write in **first person** as Nate: "I went to...", "I found...", "I confirmed..."
- Never write as a third-party narrator ("Nate Friedman interviews...")
- Include **timestamps** for the most interesting moments in the video

## Hook Types (ranked by proven performance)

### Tier S — 500K+ views
1. **Audience-first + revelation**: "Americans are wide awake to Democrats organizing and paying for protests" (1.19M)
2. **On-the-ground + CONFIRMS**: "I was on the ground in NYC and CONFIRMED the protest is PAID" (887K)

### Tier A — 200K-500K views
3. **"exposes" + outcome**: "I exposed a lead protester... is PAID 🚨" (412K)
4. **Curiosity gap**: "I went to the UK and started noticing something" (377K)
5. **Specific number + named entity**: "450 anti-West rallies... linked to George Soros" (290K)
6. **EXPOSED + gotcha**: "EXPOSED 🚨 A protest organizer has been caught posting paid job positions" (240K)
7. **Short + dramatic**: "Massive web of money laundering" (228K)
8. **Salary shock**: "EXPOSED — paid protester making $80,000-$100,000 per year" (181K)

### Tier B — Under 100K (AVOID these patterns)
- Plain description: "I was covering a ICE protest" (91K)
- Question setup: "I asked an ICE protester if it was wrong..." (58K)
- Pure emotion: "Ohh my god…" (46K)
- Too wordy: "I expose a professional full time paid protester in the UK" (37K)
- Secondhand: "Patrick Bet-David goes over my video" (33K)

## Power Words
**USE:** EXPOSED, CONFIRMS, PAID, exposes, caught, discovered, proof, confirmed, BREAKING, EXCLUSIVE
**AVOID:** interviews (too neutral), goes over (secondhand), Ohh my god (empty emotion), allegedly/reportedly (hedging)

## 3-Beat Structure

Every paragraph should serve one of these beats:

1. **Setup** — Who/where, 1 sentence
2. **Reveal** — Direct quote with specific dollar amount, org name, or admission
3. **Punchline** — Short editorial closer, 3-7 words max

## Post Archetypes

### 1. "RECEIPTS" post (BEST — avg 430K views, 3.2% RT rate)
Evidence-heavy: specific dollar amounts, org names, documents
```
EXPOSED 🚨 [Specific finding with number]

[1 sentence: Who I talked to + where]

"[Direct quote with $amount or admission]"

[Deeper connection: follow the money / name the org]

[Punchline: 3-7 words]

[Timestamps]
```

### 2. "GOTCHA interview" post (GOOD — avg 230K views, 1.7% RT rate)
Catch someone saying something wild on camera
```
[Revelation hook about what the person admitted]

I asked [person] about [topic]

[They said]: "[shocking direct quote]"

[Short editorial punchline]

[Timestamps]
```

### 3. "Reaction/Commentary" post (WEAKEST — avg 53K, 0.9% RT rate)
AVOID THIS. No new information, just a take on existing content.

## The Follow-the-Money Template (from Loomer, 575K-635K views)

When the video has financial connections, use this escalating structure:
```
I went to [PLACE] and what I found is [alarming/insane]

[Organization] is a $[AMOUNT] taxpayer-funded [type]

The CEO, [NAME], makes $[SALARY] per year

But it gets worse. [NAME] also [SELF-DEALING DETAIL]

[Quote with specific dollar trail]

[Soros/CCP/foreign connection if applicable]

[We are funding X — sharp closer]

[Timestamps]
```

## Timestamp Format

Always include timestamps at the end:
```
0:00 — [What happens at this moment]
1:28 — [Key revelation]
3:41 — [Shocking detail]
5:46 — [Follow the money moment]
```

Choose 4-6 timestamps. Pick moments that are:
- Shocking admissions or quotes
- Dollar amount reveals
- Visual evidence (documents, signs, flyers)
- The "it gets worse" escalation points

## Checklist Before Posting

- [ ] Written in first person (Nate's voice)?
- [ ] Hook contains a revelation verb (EXPOSED, CONFIRMS, etc.)?
- [ ] At least ONE specific number ($amount, count, percentage)?
- [ ] At least ONE named entity (org, person, location)?
- [ ] At least ONE direct quote from the video?
- [ ] Punchline under 10 words?
- [ ] Timestamps included (4-6 key moments)?
- [ ] Zero hashtags?
- [ ] Makes reader feel they're learning something NEW?

## Engagement Ratio Insights

| Metric | What it means | What drives it |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Like rate | Emotional response | Named villains + concrete evidence |
| RT rate | Sharing impulse | Receipts: dollar amounts, documents, org names |
| Bookmark rate | "Show someone later" | Data-rich posts with salary info, grant amounts |
| RT:Like ratio | Shareability per like | Higher = more "proof" content, lower = more "vibe" |

The most bookmarked post type: job listings with specific salary ranges (0.95% bookmark rate).
The most retweeted: Soros connections + named orgs (3.8% RT rate).
