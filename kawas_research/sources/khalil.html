    Update on Khalil & Taffy

    Due to the criminal charges against Khalil and Taffy, they both would not have been able to testify for themselves in their student trial. It was feared that due to this their suspension would have most likely prolonged into the Spring Semester further detrimenting their academic careers. Therefore both parties came to an agreement that Khalil & Taffy will not be suspended for the Spring Semester, they will be able to withdraw and be refunded for their classes from the Fall, and the suspension will be lifted off of their student records upon graduating as long as they do not engage in any illegal activity on campus. However they still will be undergoing criminal proceedings and will continue to fight the unjust charges brought against them by CUNY administration.

    Their lawyer said something profound to us at the end of the arbitration.

    “No one should leave this room believing this is a victory for Khalil and Taffy, this is just a recognition of a reality …The things you come to accept are not the way things work and the way things should be.”

    We hope that students continue to stand up against the injustices that their classmates have endured, and will further speak out on the aggressions of the administration and the CUNY system as a whole against their student body. (And all of it’s greater implications)

    Today to enter campus, students who come to CCNY everyday were subjected to their bags being searched, and barricades were present all over the main NAC building. This paranoia and heavy security presence contributes to a culture that CUNY has created where the students are considered a threat to their own campuses. They are criminalized for pursuing their academic interests.

    Despite the intimidation around us, a message was given to us from Khalil and Taffy’s lawyer - who himself was once expelled as a student activist for being part of one of the most monumental civil rights achievements of this country; demanding the intergration of CUNY for colored and low income students in the area. ( Which started with CCNY and expanded to other colleges)

    He said to us, (~) ” As students you need to demand to be recognized as members of the academic community. You are self determining individuals who are as intelligent as the people teaching you and administrating you… we changed City College in the 60’s , and it has taken a turn for the worse, it’s up to you to change it back…We didn’t do you any favors when we gave you admissions into this college, we put you on the front lines of struggle…its up you to mobilize as students and youth to be the future of a liberated CUNY.“

    As CCNY students we have a legacy to uphold.
