23rd Jun 2017 | 2 notes

SAFE

aberkawas:
I know folks are feeling unsafe at the moment. I recognize that. I understand the way all of these events punch you in the gut and keep tension pulsing your veins.
I realize at times like these social media explodes into a stream of posts telling you how to think, react, feel, etc and it’s all very overwhelming and often times counterproductive to what you need.
Writing this out of the purest intention not to contribute to that culture but out of the spirit of conveying love and justice to my sisters:
The last thing we need in this moment is try to learn safety by people who promote unsafe environments.
Prisons are unsafe . Policing in this country is unsafe.
You can not learn safety from people who uphold institutions of violence.
Remember that interpersonal violence is perpetuated by state violence against people of color, women, immigrants, etc.- and policing institutions uphold that violence.
Saftey is first and foremost ABOLITION - transformative justice - transparency - accountability in our communities.
Safety can be found in community. With consistent communication and congregation.
Don’t belittle the power of friendship and sisterhood.
We have all of the tools of healing within us - and we can collectively empower, defend and create a haven with one another when we gather in spaces that do that for us.
The work to create these spaces is tiresome, emotionally heavy & often done most often in organic ways without compensation - it doesn’t always have to be with an organization or institution.
Often times we create these spaces with good intentions and we make mistakes along the way.  
We are not each other’s enemies- we have enough threats to our humanity to fight externally. However, that does not mean that we can’t hold ourselves accountable internally and do the hard work/ have the hard conversations for culture change to uphold a community of justice.
In the spirt of tough conversations I urge my sisters not to attend any events in the name of safety held by police or corrections officers & I urge community spaces not to rely on/increase the presence of authorities for security purposes.
How can you learn self defense or be defended from people who work in an institution that you need to defend yourself from?
We need to confront these contradictions. We cannot think of Nabra and forget Charleena .
We can’t forget the cries of Philando Castille’s 4 yr old daughter ,Diamond, pleading “Mom, please stop saying cuss words and screaming because I don’t want you to get shooted ”
What we must remember is Diamond saying to her mother in this moment of desperation  “I could keep you safe”
SAFE
Think about her when you try to define safe.
The safety I want for my sisters who feel distressed because of Nabra is the same safety I want for Philando’s finance and daughter; that I wished was there for Charleena and Sandra.
We won’t find that being taught to us by the people who work for institutions that disproportionately kill Black people in America and are not held accountable.
SAFE
We can keep each other safe. We can defend ourselves and the ones we love. In fact, we are all we have to defend ourselves.
& The safety we need is safety from the violence that the police perpetuate.
So in the name of safety, here are some recommendations & resources:
If you don’t know where to find a self-defense class taught in the spirit of upholding true safe values - I will personally refer you or assist in organizing classes for you. Some self-defense instructors & initiatives I personally recommend: Nisma Zakaria & Maryam Aziz and WISE: https://www.facebook.com/imwise.org/
What to do instead of calling the police : https://docs.google.com/document/d/1_Y0LwX0uOz-P63FVhV0OFkDObbBXcy16YPOcsqnBqto/mobilebasic
+ I suggest my sistas to find and attend youth groups/circles for women organized by local communities.
Also asking folks to resource pool in the comments below with more suggestions.

Prayers on a rainy end of November


Father Mother God, thank You for Your presence during the hard and mean days.
For then we have You to lean upon.

Thank You for Your presence during the bright and sunny days, for then we can share that which we have with those who have less.

And thank You for Your presence during theHoly Days, for then we are able to celebrate You and our families and our friends.

For those who have no voice, we ask You to speak.

For those who feel unworthy, we ask You to pour Your love out in waterfalls of tenderness.

For those who live in pain, we ask You to bathe them in the river of Your healing.

For those who are lonely, we ask You to keep them company.

For those who are depressed, we ask You to shower upon them the light of hope.

Dear Creator, You, the borderless sea of substance, we ask You to give to all the world that which we need most – Peace.

Amen.

13th Apr 2016

Desperate Weeks

This week I’ve been thinking a lot about prison & prisoners.
So many things have alerted me about how much our bodies are owned,
how they get moved across borders,
have lines drawn over them.
In an out of jail cells.
Moving to the rhythm of the labor bell.
Last week marked a year since our sisters Noelle and Asia were arrested- entrapped by an NYPD undercover-we still don’t know their fate. On April 3rd -former Black Panther , Abdullah Majid, died in prison after spending 34 years behind bars as a political prisoner. The next day two of our  brothers ,Salem Abdu Salam  and Omar Mahjour , were released from Guantanamo Bay after 12 years of trials and fighting.While we  celebrated this busloads of approximately 100 South Asian detainees were deported; community members escaping harsh circumstances here in the U.S. only to be sent back to harsher ones in exile . These are some stories that passed me by in the last few weeks & there are so many more.
But then this weekend, I felt the deepest low. I heard the news of my brother, whose case I’ve followed & whose family I knew- Ahmad Farhani. After being pursued for years by the NYPD and eventually caught up in an entrapment case , last weekend he attempted suicide in order to escape his circumstance.
Collateral Damage
Entrapped
SO trapped
He saw nowhere out but death.
This is the state our people are in.
We feel more loss than victory- & over and over again, we are reminded that we are owned- that our fates are in the hands of others.

http://abdullahmajid.com/bro-abdul-majid-joins-the-ancestors/
https://www.rt.com/usa/320706-nypd-undercover-muslim-surveillance/
http://www.notonemoredeportation.com/2016/04/05/fate-unknown/
http://www.huffingtonpost.com/dr-maha-hilal/an-entrapped-muslim-man-attempted-suicide-but-who-cares_b_9668634.html
Prisoners are people I feel intrinsically connected to. When my father was in jail- it didn’t matter what was happening we always felt like we were chained to him. No moment felt fully alive. Even light moments were weighed down by the reality we lived in. There was always a sense of connection - a sense that you could not let him go or forget.
But that was blood connection - and I wonder if we as a community have a soul connection to our brethren in these situations. I wonder why we don’t feel so chained to the 1000’s who are behind bars now, surrounded by walls. I wonder if we think about how lonely and deprived they are in these moments. How even in their release they live in exile.
This last week tore me up. Left me heart broken, feeling dead inside.
But I still fight for control of our fates. Knowing that those behind bars have souls more free than ours. For even in their imprisonment their hearts and minds are free.
It is us who I fear for. Our mobility has blinded us. We are imprisoned in a false sense of security. We don’t care. We forget. We forget who the enemy is. The enemy who places the shackles on our people.
When we release the mental shackles that hold us down, maybe we’ll actually be able to move to free those behind bars.
Free your mind, free your people.
This feels more like a plea than anything else.
Right now I feel desperate.

- For Hijabi’s of NY ;) 

“I’m learning that there is an immense responsibility that you hold when working for a community to be inclusive and just towards the people you work for and represent. One of the most important things to realize is that if you are in a position to serve your community then you are probably more privileged than most of those you serve. Therefore, you must always remember to step out of yourself and uplift the voices of the most vulnerable, impacted, and marginalized- especially when it comes to combatting Islamophobia.

Recently I’ve had the chance to speak on several well known news outlets- they label me “ activist”, “leader”, “feminist” etc. but I wonder a lot about what all of this means. Although I am ultimately appreciative of having a chance to put a positive narrative of Muslims into the mainstream, I am also critical of the projection and influence that certain narratives normalize. The fact that the only questions I get asked about by media are about my experience wearing hijab, job discrimination, and bullying means that we are talking around the dire reality that our community is facing. When we constantly focus on individual cases of discrimination rather than addressing the systematic racism embedded in the policies targeting our community - we are throwing the most impacted and least visible victims of Islamophobia under the bus. There are wars going on, families are being droned to death, and people are being surveilled. At this present moment Muslims are being killed, jailed, deported, entrapped, and have been for over a decade.

Many “leaders” in our community want to brush these stories under the rug and fight Islamophobia by creating an image of the Muslim community as a middle class model minority - who are just like everyone else. There is a very prevalent “we can prove ourselves” narrative being perpetuated in our community in an effort to be accepted by a society that is constantly rejecting us. However, the reality is that not all of us are accepted, or will be. We’re not a perfect community. We don’t all act perfect, speak eloquently, or look the part. In fact some of us have criminal records, drop out of school, are living off of food stamps and are just struggling to survive- this doesn’t mean that any of us deserve to be dehumanized.

This is besides the millions abroad who remain nameless and voiceless. We dehumanize them by talking about them as talking points and statistics but not like they are our brothers and sisters whose fates are inherently tied to ours. Who’s fighting for them? When did combatting Islamophobia stop being about ending wars, freeing the prisoners, and counteracting violent policies systematically targeting communities? Do we all have to be CEO’s, invent amazing things, have prestigious degrees, and be involved in the community to deserve humanity?

I believe that all of our experiences are significant, and all of our voices and stories need to be heard, but in the mainstream some stories are preferred over others. However, the Quran teaches us to uplift the oppressed and not to leave them behind. I am at a place where I have so many privileges that allow me a voice and acceptance in spaces where so many in my community do not have access to. So what kind of leader, activist, or feminist do I want to be? Well, I want to put my efforts where they are needed. I want to serve the forgotten ones in the community, the neglected ones, the ones who are not in our newsfeeds every day, but are suffering silently. I pray that I have the opportunity to do so, and that I don’t leave them behind as I move forward."

2nd Apr 2016

HOPE:

COR Reflection 1: Where are you finding hope these days?
Over the past few months, but I guess really since ever- I’ve been feeling up and down. My ups and downs revolve through time; I have happy moments throughout the day, only to feel low at night. Some weeks fly by without a hinge while others drag on and leave me struggling to recuperate. Within the same hour or moment I can be feeling utter happiness , but still have a knot at the pit of my stomach. 
I’ve been living in this dualism. 
Although each day I find a new sense of growth and fulfillment, as I grow I also feel pain. Pain that I’ve always had inside but also new kinds of pain I never thought I would feel. Sometimes it’s personal, sometimes it’s for my people & really to me they are one in the same.
I believe this pain grows as I become more knowledgeable about the world and what I owe it. The more I learn about organizing people intentionally, about justice, about social transformation- the more frustrated I become with the systems I see, the systems I work out of, and the systems that bind me and my people.This pain also grows the more I witness and become aware of injustice- how deeply embedded it is & how blind we’ve become to it. 
But once you see- you cannot un-see. & When I see injustice I feel it. It angers me, sometimes it severely depresses, numbs, and paralyzes me. I’ve been trying to cope with constantly being pushed down by these feelings while I have to get up every day and move forward.
However throughout all of this, and as I think about hope, I realize that although I’ve had ups and downs- I have never lost hope. In fact hope lives inside of me regardless of whatever emotional condition I am in whether it be of hardship or ease. I am thinking about what the feelings of hardship and ease mean to me and how they are indicators of hope.
DUALISM: HARDSHIP & EASE
I’m finding hope in our frustration. When we are feeling at odds it is because our bodies, minds, and souls are yearning for the liberation they deserve. That anxiety, that weight, that anger that swells up inside of us is what feeds us the fuel to fight. It is what tells us that the current state of things is wrong.
 I am finding hope in the fact that this hurts. I am finding hope in knowing that when we are sad it is because we know there is a better world out there and we can be living in it. When we are triggered because we feel the trauma inside that infuriates us - it means that we are still angry, we have not accepted or normalized injustice, it means that we are challenging it- even by the flow of our blood.
I also find hope in the moments that we feel at ease - because they are a glimpse of the world we want people to experience everyday.
The true love of brotherhood/sisterhood-creating family. The warmth of a smile that comes from genuine connection. The overwhelming energy we feel when we gather as crowds or sit in circles to heal. The small moments of peace that we feel in safe spaces, because the spaces are established with the intentions of justice. Once we are in these spaces we know that if we can create justice in a room, or in a relationship, or in an movement- then there is a possibility to create justice in this world. 
These are the moments that we feel the tangible effects of the things we fight for every day but may never actually see. I find hope in the fact that we are raising hell, but still  having fun doing it. The fact that we can find joy in the spaces that we explore our oppression in is hopeful to me.
So, in this dualism ultimately I find hope in feeling. The fact that we can simultaneously be feeling the pain and fulfillment of resisting means we are alive in a world trying to dehumanize us. So whether these feelings are of hardship or ease - it matters that we feel. 
Therefore we are, therefore we deserve more, therefore we will fight for it, and therefore we will win.

Descisions

No one ever warns you
about how difficult it will be
To chase your dreams
When you grow up witnessing
Every adult you know
Give up their dreams for survival

They had their dreams crushed
By a land called America
& Some came with no dreams at all
Some never even knew how

& here you are
They say the world is at your feet
& all you have to do is make the right choices
But you are paralyzed
Because you don’t even know what that means
To have choice
To be encouraged
To believe that what you want can be yours

And no one warns you
of how easy it is to surrender
To what you’ve always known
Instead of walking into an abyss
Of the things you could never imagine
Because you always saw them being denied

The self help books, 
the planners, 
& the life goal maps
have no significance
when you are still enveloped in disbelief.
